Cost of Living Across Australian States

Introduction

The cost of living in Australia varies across states, with different factors affecting the overall index, such as mortgage prices, rent, groceries, fuel, and electricity costs. To provide a comprehensive understanding of how these costs differ, the "Generalized Cost of Living Index" was created. This index normalizes the values across different categories to provide a composite score for each state.

The Generalized Cost of Living Index is calculated using the Min-Max Normalization method, which rescaled the values for each category (Mortgage, Rent, Groceries, Fuel, Electricity) to a range between 0 and 1. This makes the values comparable across the different categories, ensuring consistency.

Categories Used:
Mortgage: Median house prices in each state's capital city.
Rent: Average weekly rent for both units and houses in each city.
Groceries: Average cost of various grocery items (e.g., milk, eggs, chicken breast) for each city.
Fuel: Weekly fuel costs for each capital city.
Electricity: Quarterly electricity bills for each state.

The overall Generalized Cost of Living Index is derived by averaging the normalized values for each state across all categories.

Property, Loans, and Mortgages

Property prices, loan commitments, and mortgage rates are crucial elements of the cost of living. This section provides insights into how property values have changed over time and how they differ across states. Loan commitments reflect both economic activity and individual purchasing power, which are influenced by property prices and interest rates.

The connection between rising property prices and increased loan commitments is clear—states with higher property values typically see more people taking out mortgages, further escalating living costs. The pressure on the housing market, especially in larger cities, contributes significantly to financial strain, making affordability a critical issue.

Conclusion

The data presented across these visualizations highlights the complexities of the cost of living in Australia. From housing prices and loan commitments to everyday essentials like food and transport, each factor plays a critical role in shaping the financial landscape across different states. High population areas like New South Wales and Victoria face the greatest challenges, driven by skyrocketing housing costs and a competitive loan market, whereas smaller states and rural regions maintain relatively lower costs.

By examining these patterns over time, we gain a deeper understanding of how economic policies, interest rates, and property markets influence living standards. These insights help to anticipate future trends, allowing policymakers, businesses, and individuals to better navigate the evolving cost of living in Australia.
